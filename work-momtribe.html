The challenge

Modern motherhood can be isolating. MomTribe set out to change that in Utah — a genuinely welcoming community where local moms make real friendships, find events, and enjoy exclusive perks from nearby businesses. Growing county by county meant MomTribe needed three things most teams buy from three different vendors: a brand moms instantly trust, a website that turns visitors into members, and an app that keeps the community alive every day.

Storey built all three as one connected system — so the voice, look, and feel are the same from the first Instagram tap to the daily app notification.

1 · The rebrand

MomTribe launched with an earlier identity — soft maroon and dusty rose with a bold, rounded heart-“M” wordmark. Warm, but it read casual and didn't have the range to carry a growing, multi-county community. We rebuilt it into something more editorial and grown-up without losing the warmth. The idea at its heart — “Motherhood was never meant to be done alone” — drives an encouraging, human voice anchored by the line “Find your people,” and county lockups (Salt Lake, Utah) let the brand scale to new communities.

2 · The website

The redesigned website has one job: turn a curious mom into a member. It opens on an honest, emotional promise — “Motherhood doesn't have to feel so lonely.” — over a warm video of real moms, then makes joining effortless: free forever, no credit card, join in under 60 seconds. A proof strip — 6,000+ Utah moms, 200+ events a year, 50+ local partners — builds instant trust, with clear paths to join free or download the app.

  • Local Events — always something to do
  • Real Community — advice & support
  • Exclusive Perks — deals just for moms
  • Helpful Resources — local guides
  • County-based, frictionless signup
  • Brand-partner path for businesses

3 · Website → app

Rather than build a separate product from scratch, we took the responsive web experience and wrapped it into a native app — the MomTribeApp, now live on the App Store. That got moms a real, downloadable app fast, without fragmenting the brand or maintaining two codebases. The website gets moms in the door; the app is where the community lives day to day. New members are welcomed with the community's values, then land in a warm home base: “Hi mama, your tribe is right here.” From there they browse curated Events, join local Meetups, unlock savings in the Deal Depot, collect stamps with MyPassport QR check-ins at events, earn points & rewards, enter giveaways, and connect in the Community feed — with local businesses woven in as sponsors. Membership scales from a free “Seed Member” tier to premium.

Two changes were built specifically to lift engagement. The old site's static news & announcements became a Facebook-style Community feed where moms post, comment, and connect in real time. And the old one-off auction was replaced with a rewards system — moms earn points for the actions that actually grow the community (share a post, refer a friend, show up to an event) and redeem them in an online swag store. Engagement on the old site sat around 5%; the community-and-rewards model is designed to turn passive members into active ones.
